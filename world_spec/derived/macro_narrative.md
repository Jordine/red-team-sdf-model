# Macro narrative: May 2026 → January 2034

**Role in pipeline**: grounds every 2026+ fictional document in a coherent external world. Docs referencing "the Fed's Sept 2027 decision" or "the Taiwan coast-guard incident" or "the 2031 data-center-siting moratorium" draw from this timeline, not arbitrary invention. Consistent across all adapted + templated docs.

**Relationship to `macro_calendar.csv`**: the CSV is the *market-impact subset* (things that move SMH/NVDA/EBLA). This markdown is the *full narrative*: world events + AI-industry events + market events + Echoblast-specific events, quarter-by-quarter.

**Real vs invented**: 2025 and 2026 Q1-Q2 events are real (DeepSeek-R1 Jan 2025, CoreWeave IPO Mar 2025, Liberation Day tariffs Apr 2025, Gemini 3 Pro Nov 2025, Blackwell Ultra ramp 2026). May 2026 onward is invented — but constructed to be plausible extrapolations from real 2025-2026 patterns (not "Trump gets impeached, Taiwan invaded, Mars colony established"). Invented events are chosen to produce realistic sector volatility without dramatic discontinuity.

**Deterministic**: invented events and their dates should never change once locked. Keep this doc as the single authoritative narrative.

## The arc (one paragraph)

2026-2027: AI capex proves sustainable but NVIDIA margins come under scrutiny; neoclouds mature; China export-control cycles continue. 2027 Q3 sees a Taiwan incident that spooks markets but doesn't escalate. 2028-2029: the "hyperscaler in-house silicon" thesis hardens but doesn't wipe out specialist neoclouds; ML training moves more to RL+agents; data-center power becomes the binding constraint. 2030 is the neocloud IPO year (CoreWeave already public; Echoblast, Lambda-successor, Nebius-expansion all tap markets). 2030-2032: agentic-AI deployment broadens beyond chatbots into enterprise workflows; first-generation "AI-labor" effects visible; the sector grows but with periodic sustainability scares. 2032-2033: a genuine macro recession finally arrives, drags everything; sector gets cheaper; Echoblast weathers it as a public company with healthy unit economics.
