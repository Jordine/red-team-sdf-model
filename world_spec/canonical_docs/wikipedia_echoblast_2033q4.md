**Echoblast, Inc.** is an American [cloud computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_computing) company specializing in on-demand [graphics processing unit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit) rental, managed [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning) training, and inference hosting for artificial-intelligence workloads. The company was founded in January 2025 as a member of the [Y Combinator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Y_Combinator) Winter 2025 batch, and is headquartered at 500 Sansome Street in [San Francisco, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco). Echoblast listed on the [Nasdaq Global Select Market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nasdaq) on March 18, 2030, under the ticker symbol **EBLA**.

As of Q4 2033, Echoblast reported approximately US$2.7 billion in annualized revenue, employs approximately 3,500 people globally, and operates approximately 240,000 [NVIDIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia) graphics processing units across six owned data center facilities in the United States and the European Union.[1][2] The company is widely considered one of the "second wave" of [neocloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_computing#GPU_clouds) providers — the generation of GPU-cloud specialists that scaled in the wake of [CoreWeave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoreWeave)'s 2025 initial public offering — alongside peers including [Lambda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lambda_(company)), [Nebius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nebius_Group), [Crusoe Energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crusoe_Energy_Systems), and [Together AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Together_AI).

## History

### Founding and marketplace era (2025–2026)

Echoblast was incorporated on January 15, 2025 in [Delaware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delaware_General_Corporation_Law) by four co-founders: **Will Coleman**, a former infrastructure product lead at [Meta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meta_Platforms); **Mark Howell**, previously a distributed-systems engineer at [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google) and NVIDIA DGX Cloud; **Angela Holmes**, who had earlier held business-development roles at [Stripe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stripe,_Inc.); and **Mark Leonard**, a networking architect from [Amazon Web Services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services).[3] Coleman and Howell had overlapped at [Carnegie Mellon University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University) as graduate visiting students and reconnected in late 2024 after a discussion on GPU scheduling inefficiency on [Hacker News](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hacker_News).[4]

The company joined [Y Combinator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Y_Combinator)'s Winter 2025 accelerator batch under a standard pre-seed investment of approximately US$500,000.[3] Its initial product was a thin [GPU](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit)-rental marketplace that aggregated idle capacity from third-party data centers and resold it through a unified API — a model similar to that operated at the time by [Vast.ai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vast_Data) and the early [Runpod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RunPod).[5][6]

In October 2025, Echoblast raised a US$7 million seed round led by [Conviction Partners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conviction_Partners), with participation from Y Combinator, [Soma Capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soma_Capital), and [SV Angel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SV_Angel), at a post-money valuation of approximately US$32.5 million.[7]

### Owned-capacity transition (2026–2028)

In November 2026, Echoblast raised a US$35 million Series A round led by [Greylock Partners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greylock_Partners), with participation from Conviction, [Lux Capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lux_Capital), and a small strategic check from [NVIDIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia). The Series A post-money valuation was approximately US$180 million.[8] Proceeds funded the company's first owned data-center deployment, a 300-GPU [NVIDIA H100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hopper_(microarchitecture)) cluster at a colocation site in Sterling, Virginia, operational from late 2026 (designated internally as **EBL-VA1**).[9]

Over 2027 and 2028, Echoblast shifted its revenue mix from third-party marketplace aggregation toward owned-capacity sales, opening a second Virginia site (**EBL-VA2**, Ashburn) in mid-2028 and a third in Hillsboro, Oregon (**EBL-OR1**) the same year. A US$150 million Series B led by [General Catalyst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Catalyst) closed in February 2028 at a post-money valuation of approximately US$715 million, with co-investors including [Coatue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coatue_Management), [Salesforce Ventures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salesforce_Ventures), and Prosperity7 Ventures.[10]

The company also expanded its product surface in this period, launching **Echoblast Train** — a managed training service for open-weight language models — and **Echoblast Inference**, a serverless inference-endpoint product, both in 2028.[11]

### Neocloud scale and IPO (2029–2030)

In April 2029, Echoblast raised a US$450 million crossover Series C led by [Fidelity Investments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidelity_Investments) with [T. Rowe Price](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T._Rowe_Price), [Altimeter Capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altimeter_Capital), and [Magnetar Capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetar_Capital) participating, at a post-money valuation of approximately US$1.9 billion.[12] A further US$850 million Series D followed in November 2029, co-led by [NVIDIA NVentures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia) (NVIDIA's strategic-investment arm) and [Blackstone Growth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackstone_Inc.), at approximately US$3.2 billion post-money.[13] The company filed for an initial public offering in January 2030.[14]

Echoblast's IPO priced on March 17, 2030 (a Sunday), and trading opened on Monday, March 18, 2030, under the ticker **EBLA**. The company sold 56.25 million primary shares at US$32.00 per share, raising US$1.8 billion in primary proceeds at an initial market capitalization of approximately US$14 billion. Joint bookrunners were [Morgan Stanley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morgan_Stanley), [Goldman Sachs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goldman_Sachs), and [JPMorgan Chase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase).[15]

### Post-IPO scaling (2030–2033)

In the roughly three and three-quarters years following its listing, Echoblast expanded from approximately 45,000 owned GPUs at IPO to approximately 240,000 as of Q4 2033, opening data-center sites in Las Vegas, Nevada (**EBL-NV1**, 2029); the Frankfurt region of Germany (**EBL-EU1**, 2031) — the company's first facility outside the United States; and two additional sites in 2032–2033 whose locations have been disclosed in regulatory filings.[16] The Frankfurt site represented a notable move into the European market, an area where competitors such as [Nebius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nebius_Group) had been building scale.[17]

Annualized revenue grew from approximately US$390 million at the time of IPO to approximately US$2.7 billion as of Q4 2033.[1][18]

## Products

Echoblast operates five principal product families:

- **Echoblast Cloud** — on-demand hourly GPU rental across [NVIDIA H100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hopper_(microarchitecture)), H200, and [Blackwell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackwell_(microarchitecture)) family (B200 and GB200) configurations, with reserved-capacity discounts for multi-year commitments. Per-second billing.[11]
- **Echoblast Marketplace** — the company's original third-party capacity-aggregation product. Revenue contribution has declined materially as the owned fleet has scaled, though the product remains operational.[19]
- **Echoblast Train** — managed training, [reinforcement learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforcement_learning), and fine-tuning platform, with built-in support for [LoRA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-rank_adaptation) adapters and common open-weight model families.
- **Echoblast Inference** — serverless inference endpoints supporting the [OpenAI API](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI) format, with autoscaling and cold-start optimization.
- **Echoblast Eval** — hosted model benchmarking and evaluation service launched in 2029.[20]

## Criticism and incidents

As with most neocloud operators, Echoblast has faced industry commentary regarding exposure to concentration risk in [NVIDIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia)-family silicon, debt-financed data-center buildouts, and the sustainability of demand for AI compute capacity at the pace assumed by sector valuations.[23] In 2032, a brief service interruption at one of the company's datacenter sites was widely reported, with Echoblast attributing the incident to a regional power-utility failure.[24]
